\section{Summary}

The current Furuta pendulum model uses the updated \texttt{arm\_2.STL} and
\texttt{rod\_2.STL} CAD files.  The no-domain-randomization (no-DR) policy
balances well in clean simulation, but it is fragile when tested under
realistic perturbations.  The current deployment candidate is
\texttt{real\_ready\_stage2}, a Stage 2 domain-randomized (DR) policy.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrrrrr}
\toprule
\textbf{Policy tested under Stage 2 DR} & \textbf{Mean} & \textbf{Std} & \textbf{Worst} & \textbf{Best} & \textbf{Success} \\
\midrule
No DR baseline & 1729.64 & 1336.33 & 84.20 & 2893.91 & 60\% \\
Stage 2 DR & 2554.67 & 43.38 & 2483.71 & 2638.26 & 100\% \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Key result:} when both policies are tested under the same Stage 2
randomized conditions, Stage 2 improves worst-case reward from 84.20 to
2483.71.  This is the main sim-to-real robustness result.

After Stage 2, the reward was tightened for longer upright balance.  The best
current simulation candidate is the saved elbow-disturbance checkpoint
\texttt{20260605\_elbow\_kick\_0p5deg\_x3\_fall20\_longrun/best\_model.zip},
which survived a 60 s recorded episode with three small elbow angle kicks and
scored 8240.4 / 8400.

\section{RL Environment and Training}

The policy observation is
\[
\mathbf{o}=[\cos\theta,\ \sin\theta,\ \dot\theta,\ \phi,\ \dot\phi].
\]
The action is normalized shoulder motor torque.  The original dense reward is
\[
r=\cos(\theta)-0.1u^2-0.5\max(0,\ |\phi|-2.094)^2.
\]
Episodes last 30 s (3000 control steps).  The reset state is near the hanging
position, $\theta\approx-\pi$, with small random noise.  PPO from
Stable-Baselines3 is trained with \texttt{VecNormalize}; DR policies warm-start
from the clean no-DR baseline.

For long-hold training, the reward was extended with upright bonuses and a
small velocity penalty near upright:
\[
r=\cos(\theta)-0.1u^2-\text{limit penalty}
  +0.2\mathbf{1}_{|\theta|<10^\circ}
  +0.2\mathbf{1}_{|\theta|<5^\circ}
  -0.005\dot{\theta}^2\mathbf{1}_{|\theta|<10^\circ}.
\]
Once the pendulum first reaches $|\theta|<10^\circ$, the episode is considered
captured.  Later runs terminate only if $|\theta|>20^\circ$ after capture,
allowing recovery from small disturbances while still rejecting full falls.

\section{Stage 2 Domain Randomization}

The current default DR profile is \texttt{real\_ready\_stage2}.  It keeps the
good parts of earlier DR tests and excludes elbow velocity filtering.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Parameter} & \textbf{Range} & \textbf{Reason} \\
\midrule
Mass/inertia & $\pm5\%$ & CAD/measurement uncertainty \\
Shoulder damping & $\pm5\%$ & gearbox/friction variation \\
Elbow damping & $[0,\ 0.00002]$ Nm s/rad & tiny pivot friction \\
Motor gear/torque & $\pm5\%$ & voltage/motor variation \\
Action delay & 0--1 step & timing margin \\
Shoulder pos. noise & $\sigma=0.017$ rad & backlash / encoder error \\
Shoulder vel. noise & $\sigma=0.050$ rad/s & velocity estimate uncertainty \\
Elbow pos. noise & $\sigma=0.001$ rad & AS5600 angle noise \\
Shoulder zero offset & $\pm1^\circ$ & calibration tolerance \\
Elbow zero offset & $\pm0.5^\circ$ & upright zero tolerance \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Elbow velocity filtering is excluded because it adds phase lag to
$\dot\theta$.  A narrow filter range can be tolerated, but broad filtering
reduced worst-case performance.

\section{Elbow Friction and Sensor Lessons}

The updated rod is light, so elbow damping must be very small.  The old range
$[0,\ 0.003]$ Nm s/rad was too large: at 10 rad/s it produces 0.030 Nm of
damping torque, about 4.5 times the rod gravity torque scale.

\section{Long-Hold and Disturbance Training}

The Stage 2 policy was further fine-tuned to reduce fall-and-recover behavior.
The main changes were 60 s episodes, upright bonuses inside 10$^\circ$ and
5$^\circ$, a small $\dot{\theta}$ penalty near upright, and post-capture
termination if the pendulum leaves a 20$^\circ$ safety envelope.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
\textbf{Run} & \textbf{Disturbance} & \textbf{Best eval} & \textbf{Comment} \\
\midrule
\texttt{hard\_hold\_10deg\_ent001} & none & 7443.74 / 8400 & 60 s hard-hold policy \\
\texttt{elbow\_kick\_2deg\_fall20} & 1 kick, $\pm2^\circ$ & unstable & Too harsh for PPO \\
\texttt{elbow\_kick\_1deg\_fall20} & 1 kick, $\pm1^\circ$ & unstable & Degraded during eval \\
\texttt{elbow\_kick\_0p5deg\_x3} & 3 kicks, $\pm0.5^\circ$ & 8233.1 / 8400 & Best disturbance checkpoint \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The best disturbance run peaked at 1.93M steps with reward 8233.1 $\pm$ 38.1
and full 6000-step episodes.  Continuing training past the peak degraded the
latest policy, so the saved \texttt{best\_model.zip} is used rather than the
final checkpoint.  A 60 s GIF from this saved best model scored 8240.4 / 8400.

\section{Interpretation and Next Steps}

The no-DR policy is excellent in clean simulation but fails in some randomized
episodes.  Stage 2 DR sacrifices a small amount of nominal performance but is
far more consistent under realistic imperfections.  Clean simulation can look
stationary after balance because no noise or disturbance pushes the pendulum
away from upright; with disturbances, the controller does react.

Next steps:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Evaluate the saved elbow-kick best model across many fixed seeds and
          report success rate, not only a single GIF.
    \item Measure real elbow free-swing decay to identify pivot friction.
    \item Measure AS5600 velocity-estimator lag before adding velocity filtering to DR.
    \item Test the hard-hold policy first on hardware after AS5600 upright zero calibration.
    \item Add board tilt / external disturbance DR after stationary transfer is validated.
\end{enumerate}
